Sri Lanka, is a thriving biodiversity hotspot with a diverse array of ecosystems and endemic species. Nestled between Southeast Asia and Africa, the island's unique flora and fauna make it a sanctuary for numerous species. The Asian elephant, a revered species, is found in protected areas like Udawalawe and Minneriya National Parks. The island also boasts a diverse array of ecosystems, including the UNESCO World Heritage Site Sinharaja Forest Reserve and the arid plains of Yala National Park. The island also boasts a rich marine ecosystem, with coral reefs, dolphins, and whales. The Knuckles Mountain Range and central highlands are home to a variety of reptiles and amphibians, while the flora, including endemic orchids and rhododendron trees, is a botanical treasure trove.
